D&D 5e → 5.5e

A concise guide to the 2024 rules for players transitioning from 5e — what's new, what's changed, and what still works exactly like you remember.

Updated Apr 2026 Version 1.0 Source 2024 PHB · DMG · MM
01

Quick start

The short version: what to know before session 1.

Can I use my 5e character?

Yes, with minor adjustments:

  • Add 1 free feat to match 5.5e power level
  • Use 5.5e rules for Surprise, Inspiration/Hero Points, and new actions
  • Review the class section for changed features

Biggest changes at a glance

No racial ability bonuses

Choose where your +2/+1 (or +1/+1/+1) go — no fixed racial stats.

Level 1 feat for everyone

Every background grants an Origin Feat at creation.

Weapon Mastery

Weapons have tactical properties — Cleave, Topple, Vex, etc.

Widespread rebalancing

Small improvements across every class, spell, and subsystem.

02

Character creation

Species, backgrounds, and feats — all reworked.

Species Major

Ability score increases

  • 5e: Fixed by race (e.g., Dwarf = +2 CON, +2 WIS)
  • 5.5e: Choose +2/+1 or +1/+1/+1 to any abilities

Languages

  • Pick any 2 languages besides Common (no restrictions)
  • Total languages reduced from 16 → 10

Added to core PHB

  • Aasimar
  • Goliath
  • Orc (as its own species)

Removed

  • Half-Elf → play Elf or Human
  • Half-Orc → play Orc or Human

Backgrounds Buffed

Every background now grants an Origin Feat at level 1 — in addition to flavor, skills, and features.

03

Combat & actions

New named actions and an updated action economy.

Study New

INT check to learn about a creature/object. Uses Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, or Religion. Similar to Pathfinder's Recall Knowledge.

Influence Revised

Formalized social action using Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation, or Performance.

Magic action Terminology

Replaces "cast a spell." Includes magic items and magical abilities. Action Surge can no longer be used for Magic actions.

08

Surprise & conditions

Simpler surprise, new status effects, unified exhaustion.

Surprise Changed

5e

  • Surprised creatures skip turn 1
  • No reactions until turn 1 ends

5.5e: Surprised condition

  • Disadvantage on Initiative rolls
  • Lasts until end of your first turn

Hero Points Optional

  • Start: 5 per session · Max: 10
  • Spend 1 → reroll a d20 (keep higher)
  • Spend 1 → +10 to a d20 (after seeing result)
  • Spend 5 → stabilize at 0 HP with 1 HP
  • DM awards more for great roleplay / heroic moments

New & changed conditions

Burning New

1d4 fire at start of turn. Remove by dropping prone & rolling, dousing, or submerging.

Dehydration New

Drinking less than half required water → 1 level of Exhaustion.

Incapacitated Changed

5e effects plus: can't speak, auto-fail STR/DEX saves, auto-Surprised, breaks concentration.

Exhaustion Reworked

Exhaustion was completely streamlined. Instead of six different effects stacked on top of each other, 5.5e applies a single unified penalty that scales with each level.

5e (old)

  • L1: Disadvantage on ability checks
  • L2: Speed halved
  • L3: Disadvantage on attacks & saves
  • L4: HP max halved
  • L5: Speed reduced to 0
  • L6: Death

5.5e (new)

  • Each level: −2 penalty to all d20 tests and spell save DCs
  • Penalties stack up to −10 at L5
  • L6 still = death
  • Remove 1 level per long rest

  • Round down by default unless a feature says "round up"
  • Teleportation never provokes opportunity attacks; equipment comes with; fails if blocked

09

Rolling & mechanics

The d20 core is unchanged; small clarifications around it.

D20 tests

  1. Ability checks — doing tasks
  2. Saving throws — resisting effects
  3. Attack rolls — hitting targets

All: roll d20 + modifiers vs. DC or AC.

Advantage / Disadvantage Same

  • Roll 2d20, take higher/lower
  • Multiple sources don't stack
  • Adv + Dis = straight roll

Critical hits Slight

  • Nat 20 = crit, double damage dice
  • Some Mastery properties interact with crits

Proficiency bonus Same

  • L1–4 +2 · L5–8 +3
  • L9–12 +4 · L13–16 +5
  • L17–20 +6

Social interaction

Influence action New

Formalized as a specific action in combat or social scenarios. Use Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation, or Performance. DM sets the DC; outcome can grant advantage/disadvantage on future interactions.

13

Conversion guide

Five steps to bring a 5e character into 5.5e.

  1. Species: keep unless Half-Elf/Half-Orc; reassign ASI (+2/+1 or +1/+1/+1); pick any 2 languages + Common.
  2. Background: add an Origin Feat (or just add 1 feat).
  3. Class: check your class section; pick mastered weapons if applicable; update spell list.
  4. Actions: learn Study, Magic terminology, and Weapon Mastery.
  5. Conditions: learn Burning, Dehydration; relearn Surprise and the new Exhaustion track.
14

FAQ

Is my 5e character weaker than a 5.5e character?

Slightly — that's why you add a free feat when converting.

Can I keep using my 5e PHB?

Mostly yes, but you'll miss new options and changed features.

Do I need to buy new books?

Not required. The 2024 PHB consolidates the current rules.

What about adventures written for 5e?

They still work. DM converts monster stats and NPC abilities as needed.

Are 5e subclasses still legal?

Ask your DM. Many work fine; some are outdated.

What happens if rules conflict?

5.5e takes precedence. When in doubt, ask DM.
